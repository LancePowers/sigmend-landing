The Hidden Cost of Mental Health

The estimated economic impact to the US of serious mental illness alone is estimated to be around $193,000,000,000, but we currently have no accurate way of knowing the true cost of mental illness.

Most people know the way it feels to try to work after a bad break up, or when someone they love is really sick. Those feelings often come and go for a healthy individual. It's estimated that one in five adults experience a mental illness in a given year. Half of us will experience it in our lifetime, and one of every twenty five people suffer from a serious mental illness in a given year

It's often easier to think about the problem in terms of physical health. For example, what is the cost of one out of every five employees getting pneumonia? or if one out of every twenty-five was living with lung cancer in a given year? How much does that increase if they don't receive treatment?

Workplace Development

Assessment and Measurement

It's impossible to solve a problem without knowing what changes your efforts are making. We survey human resources, executives, and employees to better understand the current state of mental health and channels to help within your company.

This creates a baseline for future tests to better understand the best way to help your employees. Even with a very powerful tool like the assessment, there are still limits to our ability to understand the problem.

That's why Sigmend Open Source is dedicated to finding ways to collect more accurate, timely data so we can continually discover new ways of understanding the effect of mental illness on productivity and the way we function in our lives.

Empathy and Communication

We partner with organizations from the mental health community working on the frontlines of suicide prevention and serious mental illness. All of our clients participate in a Design Thinking Workshop to unify the experience of our mental health community with the innovation of our startup community.

The mental health organizations leave the workshop with tangible, actionable steps to help them better accomplish their goal. A fundamental aspect of these Design Thinking workshops is to better understand and build empathy around the problem and its stakeholders. Theses workshops are designed to prepare employees for conversations about mental illness in their own community.

Resources and Education

We work with companies to create safer, healthier workplaces with clear channels to help for employees who need it. We begin by teaching critical skills like assessing risk of suicide or harm, and what to do if you believe someone is suffering from a mental illness.

We work with our clients to create a playbook for Mental Health with access to external resources and internal policies to best assist an employee dealing with a mental illness. We provide practical solutions and clear channels to help for their employees.

Community and Awareness

Our workplace development extends beyond the course of our initial implementation. We understand the stigma around mental illness cannot be easily fixed by policies and procedures alone. Through the Sigmend Speaks program we help individuals continue the conversation, and find support and comfort in understanding they are not alone.

Our worplace development program is designed for tech companies. As a whole we are more likely to suffer from mental illnes, but we are also far more likely to take action and do something about it. Through consistent engagement and relationships that extend beyond a simple implementation. We provide on-going support to program participants to build a work environment where mental illness is both accepted and understood. Together we are building a larger, more effective, empowered mental health community!
